1. SEATED CHEST OPENER
   Sanskrit Name: Sukhasana Variation
   Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
   Duration: 3 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Counteract slouching and open the chest.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Pectorals
   • Anterior Deltoids
   • Thoracic Spine
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Reverses rounded shoulders
   • Improves breathing capacity
   • Relieves upper back tension
   • Promotes upright awareness
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Sit in a comfortable cross-legged position or on a chair with feet flat.
   2. Interlace your fingers behind your back (or hold a strap if tight).
   3. Inhale and gently straighten your arms, rolling your shoulders back and down.
   4. Lift your chest slightly toward the ceiling but keep your chin level.
   5. Breathe deeply into the upper chest for 5-8 breaths.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Jutting the chin forward/neck strain
   ✗ Arching the lower back excessively
   ✗ Forcing the hands up too high causing shoulder pain
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Acute shoulder injury (rotator cuff)
   • Wrist pain (modify by holding strap)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Imagine a string pulling your sternum to the sky"
   • "Keep your neck long and loose"
   • "Soften your jaw"

2. SPHINX POSE
   Sanskrit Name: Salamba Bhujangasana
   Difficulty: Beginner
   Duration: 4 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Gentle thoracic extension to reverse kyphosis.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Lower Back
   • Middle Back
   • Chest
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Strengthens spine
   • Opens chest/lungs
   • Soothes nervous system
   • Mild lower back compression (therapeutic)
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Lie on your belly with legs extended, tops of feet pressing down.
   2. Place elbows under shoulders, forearms parallel like number 11.
   3. Press forearms down to lift chest away from the floor.
   4. Draw shoulders away from ears and look straight ahead.
   5. Hold for 1-3 minutes, breathing softly.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Sinking into shoulders (turtle neck)
   ✗ Clenching glutes too tight
   ✗ Looking up too high (neck compression)
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Testing pregnancy
   • Acute lower back injury
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Press down through your elbows"
   • "Pull your heart forward through the gate of your arms"

3. WALL ANGEL (MOUNTAIN VARIATION)
   Sanskrit Name: Tadasana w/ Wall
   Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
   Duration: 3 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Feedback for spinal alignment.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Upper Back
   • Neck
   • Shoulders
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Incredible feedback for alignment
   • Strengthens deep neck flexors
   • Resets shoulder position
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Stand with your back against a wall, feet 6 inches away.
   2. Ensure hips, shoulder blades, and back of head touch the wall.
   3. Bring arms to a "W" shape (cactus arms) against the wall if possible.
   4. Slide arms up and down slowly while keeping contact.
   5. Focus on keeping the ribcage knitted in (not flaring).
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Letting ribs pop out to touch the wall
   ✗ Chin lifting to touch head back
   ✗ Holding breath
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Frozen shoulder (limit range)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Feel the wall supporting your spine"
   • "Keep your chin tucked slightly"
   • "Don't force the arms, go where comfortable"

4. CAT-COW FLOW
   Sanskrit Name: Marjaryasana-Bitilasana
   Difficulty: All Levels
   Duration: 5 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Mobilize the entire spine.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Spine
   • Neck
   • Core
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Lubricates spinal joints
   • Improves proprioception
   • Relieves stiffness
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Start on all fours, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips.
   2. Inhale: Drop belly, lift chest and gaze (Cow).
   3. Exhale: Press hands down, round spine, tuck chin (Cat).
   4. Move slowly with your own breath count.
   5. Repeat for 10-15 cycles.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Moving too fast
   ✗ Bending elbows excessively
   ✗ Collapsing in the mid-back
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Wrist pain (use fists)
   • Knee pain (pad knees)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Move vertebra by vertebra"
   • "Imagine a wave traveling through your spine"

5. SEATED NECK RELEASE
   Sanskrit Name: Greeva Sanchalana
   Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
   Duration: 3 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Release tension in upper trapezius.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Neck
   • Upper Traps
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Reduces tension headaches
   • Lowers stress
   • Improves neck range of motion
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Sit tall. Drop right ear toward right shoulder.
   2. Extend left arm out to side and down for deeper stretch.
   3. Hold for 5 breaths. Switch sides.
   4. Optional: Gently place hand on head (add weight, do not pull).
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Pulling the head aggressively
   ✗ Lifting the shoulder to the ear
   ✗ Holding breath
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Acute neck disk issue (herniation)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Let gravity do the work"
   • "Soften your jaw"

6. WIDE-KNEE CHILD'S POSE
   Sanskrit Name: Balasana
   Difficulty: All Levels
   Duration: 5 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Decompress the lumbar spine.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Lower Back
   • Hips
   • Glutes
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Gentle traction for lower back
   • Calms nervous system
   • Passive hip stretches
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Kneel on mat, big toes touching, knees wide apart.
   2. Sit hips back onto heels.
   3. Walk hands forward and lower chest to floor.
   4. Rest forehead on mat or block.
   5. Breathe into the back body.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Forcing hips down if knees hurt
   ✗ Tensing shoulders near ears
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Knee injury (place cushion behind knees)
   • Pregnancy (keep knees very wide)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Send your breath to your lower back"
   • "Melt your chest toward the earth"

7. SUPPORTED SUPINE TWIST
   Sanskrit Name: Supta Matsyendrasana
   Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
   Duration: 4 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Release spinal tension through rotation.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Lower Back
   • Obliques
   • Glutes
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Hydrates spinal discs
   • Relieves stiffness
   • Digestion aid
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Lie on back, hug knees to chest.
   2. Drop both knees to the right side.
   3. Extend left arm to side, gaze left.
   4. Place a pillow under knees if they don't touch floor.
   5. Hold 1-2 mins, switch sides.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Forcing knees down while lifting shoulder
   ✗ Twisting too aggressively
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • SI joint instability (keep movement small)
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Keep both shoulders grounded"
   • "Twist from the belly button"

8. ROLLING BRIDGE
   Sanskrit Name: Setu Bandhasana Flow
   Difficulty: Beginner
   Duration: 4 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Strengthen glutes/stabilize pelvis.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Glutes
   • Hamstrings
   • Core
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Stabilizes SI joint
   • Opens hip flexors
   • Strengthens posterior chain
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat hip-width apart.
   2. Inhale: Press into feet, lift hips slowly.
   3. Exhale: Lower spine vertebra by vertebra.
   4. Repeat 10 times, focusing on articulation.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Pushing too high (arching back)
   ✗ Knees splaying out
   ✗ Tensing neck
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Acute neck pain
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Lift from your hips, not your back"
   • "Squeeze glutes gently at top"

9. LEGS UP THE WALL (Back Relief)
   Sanskrit Name: Viparita Karani
   Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
   Duration: 5 minutes
   
   PRIMARY PURPOSE:
   Passive release of psoas and back muscles.
   
   TARGET AREAS:
   • Hamstrings
   • Lower Back
   
   BENEFITS:
   • Reduces inflammation
   • Relaxes psoas
   • Calms nervous system completely
   
   HOW TO PERFORM:
   1. Sit sideway against a wall.
   2. Swing legs up the wall as you lie back.
   3. Rest hips on a folded blanket if needed.
   4. Arms by sides, palms up.
   5. Close eyes and rest.
   
   COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
   ✗ Hips too far from wall (straining)
   ✗ Arching neck
   
   WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
   • Glaucoma (inversion)
   • Acid reflux
   
   INSTRUCTOR CUES:
   • "Let your thigh bones sink into your hip sockets"
   • "Do absolutely nothing"

10. SEATED QUAD SETTING
    Sanskrit Name: Dandasana Variation
    Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
    Duration: 3 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Strengthen VMO (inner quad) for tracking.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Quadriceps
    • Knees
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Improves patellar tracking
    • Non-weight bearing strength
    • Reduces knee pain
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Sit with legs extended (or one bent).
    2. Place a rolled towel under the knee being worked.
    3. Press knee down into towel to engage quad.
    4. Lift heel slightly if possible.
    5. Hold 5 seconds, release. Repeat 10x.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Holding breath
    ✗ Hyperextending
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Acute ACL tear (consult doc)
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Feel the muscle above your knee cap engage"
    • "Keep spine tall"

11. SUPPORTED GLUTE BRIDGE
    Sanskrit Name: Setu Bandhasana
    Difficulty: Beginner
    Duration: 3 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Strengthen posterior chain to offload knees.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Glutes
    • Hamstrings
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Takes pressure off knee joint
    • Stabilizes hips
    • Builds endurance
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Lie on back, feet hip-width close to glutes.
    2. Press into heels to lift hips.
    3. Ensure knees point straight (don't cave in).
    4. Hold for 3-5 breaths.
    5. Lower slowly.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Knees falling inward
    ✗ Lifting heels
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Neck pain
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Imagine a block between your knees"
    • "Drive through your heels"

12. SUPPORTED HIGH LUNGE
    Sanskrit Name: Ashta Chandrasana (Mod)
    Difficulty: All Levels
    Duration: 3 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Strengthen stabilizers.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Quads
    • Glutes
    • Calves
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Balance
    • Ankle stability
    • Hip opening
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Stand feet hip-width.
    2. Step one foot back, keeping heel lifted.
    3. Bend front knee (knee over ankle, not past).
    4. Keep back leg strong.
    5. Hold for 5 breaths.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Front knee swaying in
    ✗ Collapsing on back leg
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Acute ankle sprain
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Press the floor away"
    • "Keep your hips square to front"

14. SHOULDER FLOSSING (STRAP)
    Sanskrit Name: Shoulder Pass-Through
    Difficulty: Beginner
    Duration: 3 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Full range of motion for shoulder girdle.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Shoulders
    • Chest
    • Upper Back
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Combats frozen shoulder
    • Opens chest
    • Improves posture
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Stand tall holding a strap wider than shoulders.
    2. Inhale: Lift strap overhead.
    3. Exhale: Bring strap behind you (widen grip if needed).
    4. Inhale: Bring back up.
    5. Exhale: Lower to front.
    6. Repeat 5-10 times slowly.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Bending elbows too much
    ✗ Arching back to compensate
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Acute rotator cuff tear
    • Instability
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Keep your ribs knitted in"
    • "Move slowly through the sticky spots"

18. GENTLE SUN SALUTATION
    Sanskrit Name: Surya Namaskar A (Mod)
    Difficulty: All Levels
    Duration: 5 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Warm up entire body.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Full Body
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Circulation
    • Flexibility
    • Breath connection
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Mountain Pose: Inhale arms up.
    2. Forward Fold: Exhale, bend knees deeply.
    3. Half Lift: Inhale hands to shins, flat back.
    4. Plank: Exhale step back.
    5. Knees-Chest-Chin: Lower slowly.
    6. Cobra: Inhale gentle backbend.
    7. Down Dog: Exhale hips up.
    8. Step forward & rise.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Holding breath
    ✗ Straining back in Cobra
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Wrist pain (modify plank)
    • Vertigo (slow down)
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Move like you are moving through honey"
    • "Follow your breath"

19. DANCING WARRIOR
    Sanskrit Name: Virabhadrasana Flow
    Difficulty: Beginner
    Duration: 5 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Leg strength and side body opening.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Legs
    • Hips
    • Side Body
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Strengthens legs
    • Opens hips
    • Feels empowering
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Step feet wide, right toes turn out.
    2. Bend right knee (Warrior 2).
    3. Inhale: Reverse Warrior (reach right arm up/back).
    4. Exhale: Side Angle (forearm to thigh, left arm over).
    5. Flow 5 times, then switch.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Knee collapsing in
    ✗ Shoulders hunched
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Knee issues (less bend)
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Keep your front knee stable"
    • "Flow with grace"

20. DOWNWARD FACING DOG
    Sanskrit Name: Adho Mukha Svanasana
    Difficulty: Beginner
    Duration: 2 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Full body reset.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Calves
    • Hamstrings
    • Shoulders
    • Back
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Energizing
    • Lengths spine
    • Strengthens arms
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Hands shoulder-width, feet hip-width.
    2. Lift hips up and back.
    3. Pedal out the feet (bend one knee, then other).
    4. Press chest toward thighs.
    5. Relax head completely.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Rounding spine (bend knees instead!)
    ✗ Shoulders near ears
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • High blood pressure (short hold)
    • Carpal tunnel
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Bend your knees as much as needed to keep spine straight"
    • "Shake your head yes and no"

21. LEGS UP THE WALL (Relaxation)
    Sanskrit Name: Viparita Karani
    Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
    Duration: 10 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Parasympathetic activation.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Nervous System
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Reduces cortisol
    • Aids sleep
    • Relieves tired legs
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Sit sideways to wall.
    2. Swing legs up.
    3. Arms open wide.
    4. Cover with blanket if cold.
    5. Focus on long exhales.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Doing too much. Just relax.
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Glaucoma
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Nowhere to go, nothing to do"
    • "Let the floor hold you"

22. RECLINED BUTTERFLY
    Sanskrit Name: Supta Baddha Konasana
    Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
    Duration: 5 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Open hips and relax belly.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Hips
    • Inner Thighs
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Softens abdomen
    • Gentle hip opener
    • Emotionally grounding
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Lie on back.
    2. Soles of feet together, knees open.
    3. Place blocks/pillows under knees for support.
    4. Place one hand on heart, one on belly.
    5. Feel the breath move.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Straining groin (use props!)
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Hip injury
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Use pillows under knees to fully let go"
    • "Soften your belly"

23. CORPSE POSE
    Sanskrit Name: Savasana
    Difficulty: Beginner-Safe
    Duration: 5 minutes
    
    PRIMARY PURPOSE:
    Total integration.
    
    TARGET AREAS:
    • Mind
    • Body
    
    BENEFITS:
    • Lowers blood pressure
    • Reduces fatigue
    • Mental clarity
    
    HOW TO PERFORM:
    1. Lie flat on back.
    2. Legs extended, feet flop open.
    3. Arms by sides, palms up.
    4. Close eyes.
    5. Release all muscular control.
    
    COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
    ✗ Fidgeting
    ✗ Planning the day
    
    WHO SHOULD MODIFY/AVOID:
    • Back pain (bend knees)
    
    INSTRUCTOR CUES:
    • "Let your bones be heavy"
    • "Surrender to gravity"
